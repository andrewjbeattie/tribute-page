Itzhak "Ben" Bentov (יצחק בנטוב)

Czechoslovakia-born Israeli American scientist, inventor, mystic and author

Here's a time line of Itzhak Bentov's life

  • 1923 - Bentov was born in Humenné, Czechoslovakia.
  • 1939 - During World War II, his parents were killed in Nazi concentration camps.
  • 1943 - He narrowly escaped being sent to the camps and moved to British Palestine, first living on the Shoval kibbutz in the Negev.
  • 1948 - Despite not having a university degree, Bentov joined the Israeli Science Corps, which David Ben-Gurion incorporated into the Israeli Defense Forces one month before Israel declared statehood.
  • 1949 - Bentov designed Israel's first rocket for the War of Independence.
  • 1954 - Bentov immigrated to the United States and settled in Massachusetts.
  • 1962 - Bentov became a naturalized U.S. citizen.
  • 1967 - Bentov built the steerable heart catheter.
  • 1969 - Attracted the attention of businessman John Abele, with whom Bentov founded the Medi-Tech corporation.
  • 1977 - Authored a book "Stalking the Wild Pendulum: On the Mechanics of Consciousness", he wrote that "consciousness permeates everything."
  • 1979 - Abele and Peter Nicholas looked to grow the successful business and established Boston Scientific as a holding company to purchase Medi-Tech.
  • 1979 - Bentov tragically was killed as a passenger aboard American Airlines Flight 191 that crashed at O'Hare International Airport in Chicago.
